This is a map that displays the inaccuracies with the Mercator Projection.

The Mercator projection is a cylindrical map projection developed by Gerardus Mercator in the 16th century. It became popular due to its ability to preserve angles and shapes, making it useful for navigation. However, it distorts the size and shape of landmasses as they get farther from the equator, exaggerating the size of polar regions. This distortion has been criticized as it can lead to a Eurocentric bias and an underrepresentation of the Global South. Despite its flaws, it is still commonly used in education and navigation due to its ease of use and familiarity.
